Showers can be deceptively water-intensive—especially with older or high-flow heads.

  • Low-Flow Showerhead – 5 min 40–60 ℓ
  • Standard Showerhead – 5 min 80–100 ℓ
  • High-Flow Showerhead – 5 min 100–120 ℓ
  • Average South African Shower – 5 min 90 ℓ

Tip: A 5-minute shower with a standard head can match a full bath in water use. Swapping to low-flow fixtures or using a timer can cut usage by up to 50%.

A proper handwash should use less than 2 ℓ of water if done efficiently.

Toilet flushing is one of the highest water-use activities in a household.

  • Modern Dual-Flush – Half Flush 3 ℓ
  • Modern Dual-Flush – Full Flush 6 ℓ
  • Standard Gravity Toilet (Post-1992) 6–9 ℓ
  • Older Toilet (Pre-1992) 13–20 ℓ
  • South African Average Toilet 11 ℓ

Tip: Consider retrofitting older toilets with displacement devices or switching to dual-flush models to save up to 50% per flush.

A quick face wash should use less than 2 ℓ of water.
